About

I work as a Data Scientist at OPTX, where I build systems that help the gaming and hospitality industry make smarter decisions with their marketing spend — and prove what those decisions actually returned. That means both sides of applied data science: optimization engines that recommend the right action for each customer, and the rigorous measurement — careful experiment design, honest comparisons, defensible attribution — that can survive a skeptical stakeholder.

Before OPTX, I completed my M.S. at the University of Pennsylvania (Electrical Engineering, with coursework across machine learning, statistics, and NLP), did machine learning research on robot fault detection at Penn's GRASP Lab, and spent two years at ACT Fibernet in India, where my team's cache-optimization work saved over $120K annually for a network serving half a million users. At Penn, I also served as President of the ESE Graduate Association, building community and programming for 300 graduate students.

I care about methods that are honest about their limitations, pipelines that run without babysitting, and explaining both to non-technical audiences.

Selected work

revenue attribution · production pipeline

Multi-touch offer attribution

When customers redeem several promotional offers in a single visit, revenue can't be cleanly credited offer by offer. I designed a statistically grounded attribution system that fairly distributes credit across co-redeemed offers and anchors attributed dollars to measured incremental revenue — replacing ad-hoc, offer-by-offer credit.

causal measurement · automated pipeline

Measuring marketing event impact

Marketing events had no defensible ROI measure, and hand-curated invite lists made randomized experiments impossible. I built an automated pipeline that isolates each event's true incremental impact by comparing attendees against carefully matched comparison groups — withstanding outliers and seasonality. Replaced before/after guesswork with a measurement the business can defend.

optimization · built-in validation

Campaign optimization engine

Designed and built an engine that recommends per-customer promotional offers, combining customer segmentation with interpretable statistical modeling.

autoencoders · LSTM · time-series

Fault detection for quadrupedal robots — Penn GRASP Lab

Labeled fault data was scarce, so I chose unsupervised anomaly detection over supervised classification: autoencoders on sensor time-series improved detection accuracy by 30% and identified 96% of faults, with an LSTM classifier reaching an 81% F1 score on limb-fault classification.

regression · forecasting · pandas

Energy forecasting for 100 campus buildings

With Wharton Customer Analytics, I built a regression model forecasting energy usage across ~100 University of Pennsylvania buildings from three years of consumption data — cutting forecast error (MAPE) from 146% to 16% to support cost- and carbon-reduction decisions.

NLP · sentence transformers · tableau

Media bias in women's headlines — Distinguished Project Award

Analyzed bias in headlines about women across 186 media outlets, 16 years, and 4 countries using NLP and sentence-transformer embeddings, and built an interactive Tableau dashboard to communicate the findings. Awarded Most Distinguished Project in Correlation One's data science program (4% acceptance rate).
