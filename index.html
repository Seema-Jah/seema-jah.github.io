About

I work as a Data Scientist at OPTX, where I build systems that help the gaming and hospitality industry make smarter decisions with their marketing spend — and prove what those decisions actually achieved.

Before OPTX, I completed my M.S. at the University of Pennsylvania (Electrical Engineering, with coursework across machine learning, statistics, and NLP), did machine learning research on robotics at the Penn GRASP Lab, and worked on data projects with Wharton Customer Analytics.

I care about methods that are honest about their limitations, pipelines that run without babysitting, and explaining both to non-technical audiences.

Selected work

Recent professional work is described at the method level; project write-ups with full detail are linked where public.

revenue attribution · production pipeline

Multi-touch offer attribution

When customers redeem several promotional offers in a single visit, revenue can't be cleanly credited offer by offer. I designed a statistically grounded attribution system that fairly distributes revenue across offers based on each one's marginal contribution.

causal measurement · automated pipeline

Measuring marketing event impact

Marketing events had no defensible ROI measure, and hand-curated invite lists made randomized experiments impossible. I built an automated pipeline that isolates each event's true incremental lift by comparing attendees against carefully matched comparison groups.

optimization · built-in validation

Campaign optimization engine

Designed and built an engine that recommends per-customer promotional offers, combining customer segmentation with interpretable statistical modeling. Validation is built in from day one, so every campaign's impact can be statistically tested rather than asserted.

autoencoders · LSTM · time-series

Fault detection for quadrupedal robots — Penn GRASP Lab

Labeled fault data was scarce, so I chose unsupervised anomaly detection over supervised classification: autoencoders on sensor time-series improved detection accuracy by 30% and identified failure modes that weren't in the original label set.

regression · forecasting · pandas

Energy forecasting for 100 campus buildings

With Wharton Customer Analytics, I built a regression model forecasting energy usage across ~100 University of Pennsylvania buildings from three years of consumption data — cutting forecast error significantly and surfacing seasonal patterns for facilities planning.

NLP · sentence transformers · tableau

Media bias in women's headlines — Distinguished Project Award

Analyzed bias in headlines about women across 186 media outlets, 16 years, and 4 countries using NLP and sentence-transformer embeddings, and built an interactive Tableau dashboard to communicate findings. Awarded Most Distinguished Project by Correlation One.
